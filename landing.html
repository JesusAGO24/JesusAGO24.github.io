About my interests

Hi! I'm Jesús Guzmán Ortega, a Computer Science undergrad at Tecnológico de Monterrey, passionate about solving problems that live at the intersection of code, markets, and decision-making. I bring a blend of software engineering skills, financial intuition, and algorithmic thinking, cultivated through internships at J.P. Morgan and Unilever, real-world projects, and national-level competitive programming. I’ve had the chance to work on production trading systems, financial forecasting pipelines, and AI-powered applications—from building mobile apps to contributing to real-time equity market infrastructure. Whether I’m debugging latency-sensitive workflows or solving mathematical problems under pressure, I thrive in environments that demand precision, logic, and speed. I believe in writing clean, scalable code, communicating clearly across technical and business teams, and never stopping the learning loop. Interests Big Tech Engineering: I'm drawn to backend, infrastructure, and systems engineering roles that demand high performance and scalable architecture. I love the idea of building tools that empower millions. Quantitative Development: With strong foundations in algorithms, data, and systems, I aim to design and implement low-latency, data-driven strategies that power modern finance. Algorithmic & Electronic Trading: I’m fascinated by market microstructure, execution algorithms, and the role of software in shaping modern markets—especially in high-frequency and systematic environments. Front Office Trading: Whether it’s macro, equities, or crypto, I’m deeply interested in applying technical thinking and fast decision-making to trading roles where analysis meets action. Outside of work, I’m a national ICPC finalist, a math contest winner, and a fan of the whole motorsport world.
